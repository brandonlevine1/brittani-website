## Why Most Agents Get the First 72 Hours Wrong

The moment a contract goes fully executed, most agents do one of two things: either they fire off a single "congrats, we're under contract!" email to the buyer or seller, or they dump every piece of information into one overwhelming message that nobody reads.

Both approaches fail because they ignore the operational reality of a transaction. There are 7-12 parties who need specific information at specific times: the client, the cooperating agent, the lender, the title company, the home inspector, the appraiser (eventually), and often a home warranty rep, HOA contact, or property manager.

Top TCs treat the first 72 hours like a choreographed sequence. Each email has one job. Each goes to the right person at the right time. Nothing falls through the cracks because nothing is left to memory.

## The Exact Email Sequence: 8 Emails in 72 Hours

Here's the framework used by TCs managing 30+ files per month with fewer than 2% error rates. Adapt the timing to your market, but don't skip steps.

### Email 1: Congratulations + What's Next (Hour 0-1)

**To:** Your client (buyer or seller)
**Purpose:** Emotional validation + setting expectations

This email does three things: celebrates the milestone, sets the tone for communication frequency, and provides a bullet-point timeline of what happens next. Keep it under 200 words. Attach nothing. Link to nothing. Just orient them.

Key line to include: *"Here's what happens in the next 7 days, and what I'll need from you by [specific date]."*

### Email 2: Cooperating Agent Introduction (Hour 0-2)

**To:** The other agent (and their TC if applicable)
**Purpose:** Establish the communication channel and confirm critical dates

Include: fully executed contract attached, your preferred communication method, key dates (inspection deadline, financing contingency, closing date), and your contact info. Ask them to confirm receipt and reply with their title company contact or lender info if applicable.

### Email 3: Title/Escrow Opening (Hour 1-4)

**To:** Title company or closing attorney
**Purpose:** Open escrow and transmit the contract

Attach: executed contract, MLS sheet, commission instructions, and any addenda. Request the preliminary title commitment timeline and confirm the earnest money deadline. Ask for the file number in their reply.

### Email 4: Lender Notification (Hour 1-4)

**To:** Buyer's lender (or confirm with buyer's agent if you're on the listing side)
**Purpose:** Trigger the loan process and confirm appraisal timeline

Include: property address, contract price, closing date, inspection deadlines, and any seller concessions. Ask for their estimated appraisal order date and any documentation needs from your client.

### Email 5: Client Action Items (Hour 4-12)

**To:** Your client
**Purpose:** Deliver the specific to-do list with deadlines

This is the operational email. It includes exactly what they need to do, by when, and how. Structure it as a numbered list. Examples: "Schedule home inspection by [date]—here are 3 inspectors I recommend," or "Wire earnest money to [title company] by [date]—here are the wiring instructions (call to verify before sending)."

Never combine this with Email 1. Celebration and task lists don't belong in the same message.

### Email 6: Vendor Coordination (Hour 12-24)

**To:** Home inspector, home warranty company, or other vendors
**Purpose:** Schedule and confirm services

Send inspection availability windows, property access details, lockbox codes, and any property-specific notes (dogs on premises, tenant-occupied, etc.). Confirm the appointment in writing and request a reply confirmation.

### Email 7: Full-Party Timeline Distribution (Hour 24-48)

**To:** All parties (agent, client, lender, title)
**Purpose:** Single source of truth for all critical dates

This email contains a simple table or bullet list with every deadline: inspection, inspection resolution, appraisal, financing contingency, title commitment delivery, and closing. Everyone sees the same dates. Everyone knows the stakes.

This single email prevents more blown deadlines than any other step in the sequence.

### Email 8: First-Week Check-In (Hour 48-72)

**To:** Your client
**Purpose:** Reduce anxiety, confirm progress, address early questions

A short, warm email confirming what's been accomplished ("escrow is open, inspection is scheduled for Thursday, your lender confirmed they have everything they need") and previewing the next week. End with: *"Reply to this email with any questions—no question is too small."*

For agents managing 10 deals per month, that's 45 hours recovered—more than a full work week—just from systematizing the opening communication.

## Common Mistakes That Break the Sequence

**Mistake 1: Sending everything to everyone.** Your client doesn't need the title company's internal file number email. Your lender doesn't need your client's congratulations message. Segmentation matters.

**Mistake 2: Waiting until "everything is confirmed" to start sending.** Speed matters more than perfection here. Send Email 2 and 3 within hours, even if you're still confirming small details. You can follow up. You can't recover lost days.

**Mistake 3: No templates.** If you're writing these from scratch every time, you'll skip steps when deal volume spikes. Build the templates once. Fill in the variables. Send.

**Mistake 4: Not tracking opens or replies.** If your lender never opened Email 4, you have a problem brewing. If the cooperating agent never confirmed dates, you need to follow up—not wait until the inspection deadline passes.
