## Michigan Buyer Contingency Deadlines 2026: A Form-by-Form Breakdown

Missing a contingency deadline in Michigan does not just kill a deal—it can trigger a licensing complaint faster than a seller's attorney can draft a default notice. Michigan buyer contingency deadlines 2026 remain one of the top sources of agent liability claims filed with the Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs (LARA), and the rules have not loosened. Every calendar day written into that Michigan Association of Realtors (MAR) Purchase Agreement creates an enforceable obligation, and agents who treat those dates as suggestions are gambling with their license.

This post breaks down the specific forms, statutory authority, common failures, and broker-level audits that keep Michigan buyer-side agents out of trouble.

## The Statutory Framework Behind Contingency Enforcement

Michigan's Occupational Code, specifically MCL 339.2512, empowers LARA to discipline licensees who demonstrate "incompetence" or "negligence" in performing licensed activities. A missed deadline that harms a client falls squarely within that definition. The Michigan Administrative Code R 339.22201 further requires agents to "account for and remit in a timely manner all money or property" and to act with reasonable skill—standards that courts interpret to include timely performance of contractual obligations on a client's behalf.

**The Statute of Frauds (MCL 566.108) adds another layer: any modification to a contingency deadline must be in writing and signed by both parties.** A verbal agreement between agents to "push the inspection date back a few days" is unenforceable. If a dispute arises, the original written deadline controls, and the agent who relied on a handshake deal has no defense.

The [Michigan Association of Realtors](https://www.mirealtors.com/) publishes the standard forms that most residential transactions use. These forms create the contractual deadlines that bind your client.

## Key Forms and Where Deadlines Live

Michigan agents predominantly work with MAR-published forms. The critical contingency deadlines appear in these documents:

| Form | Contingency Type | Where the Deadline Appears |
|------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| MAR Purchase Agreement (2024 revision, still active 2026) | Inspection, Financing, Appraisal, Sale of Buyer's Property | Sections 10, 11, 12, and 13 respectively |
| MAR Addendum to Purchase Agreement | Extended or custom deadlines | Fill-in-the-blank format |
| MAR Inspection Response | Buyer's repair/credit request | Must be delivered by inspection deadline |
| MAR Seller's Response to Inspection | Seller's counter to buyer demands | Tied to response window in Section 10 |

Each section of the MAR Purchase Agreement uses a "calendar days from acceptance" formula unless parties write a specific date. The agent filling in these blanks sets the clock, and there is no automatic grace period built into any MAR form. Once a deadline passes at 11:59 PM on the stated day, the contingency either expires or triggers the next party's obligation depending on the specific language.

**Agents must understand that "business days" and "calendar days" are not interchangeable in Michigan contracts.** The MAR Purchase Agreement defaults to calendar days unless the parties specifically amend otherwise. Weekends and holidays count.

## What Happens When an Agent Fails to Comply

The consequences break into three categories: contractual, regulatory, and civil.

On the contractual side, a missed inspection contingency deadline means the buyer loses the right to object or request repairs. A missed financing contingency deadline means the buyer is obligated to close regardless of loan approval status—or forfeit the earnest money deposit. A missed sale-of-property contingency deadline can give the seller the right to accept a backup offer and deliver a "kick-out" notice.

Regulatory consequences come from LARA. Under MCL 339.2512(h), a licensee can face license suspension or revocation for negligence. The typical disciplinary path starts with a formal complaint, moves to investigation by LARA's Bureau of Professional Licensing, and can result in a consent order requiring continuing education, fines up to $10,000 per violation, or license surrender.

Civil liability is the third rail. Michigan courts have awarded damages to buyers who lost favorable purchase terms because their agent failed to deliver a timely inspection objection. The agent's errors and omissions insurance may cover the claim, but premiums spike after a payout—and some policies exclude "failure to perform administrative duties" from coverage.

## Common Mistakes Michigan Agents Make With Contingency Deadlines

Five specific errors show up repeatedly in LARA complaints and civil litigation in Michigan.

**First, agents calculate deadlines from the wrong starting point.** The MAR Purchase Agreement defines "acceptance" as the moment the last party signs, but agents sometimes count from the date the offer was submitted or the date they received verbal acceptance. If a purchase agreement is accepted on a Tuesday evening but the last signature lands Wednesday morning, the clock starts Wednesday.

Second, agents fail to deliver written notice in the manner specified by the contract. Section 21 of the MAR Purchase Agreement defines acceptable delivery methods—personal delivery, mail, fax, or electronic transmission to the address specified. A text message to the listing agent's personal phone may not satisfy the contractual delivery requirement unless that number is listed in the agreement's notice section.

Third, agents assume the listing agent's verbal acknowledgment extends a deadline. As noted above, MCL 566.108 requires written modification. The listing agent saying "no problem, take an extra day" over the phone does not amend the contract. If that listing agent later denies the conversation—or simply forgets—the buyer's contingency has lapsed.

Fourth, agents confuse the inspection contingency with the inspection itself. Scheduling the inspection is not the same as exercising the contingency. The buyer must deliver a written response (repair request, credit request, or notice of termination) by the deadline. Having the inspector on-site the day before the deadline means nothing if the written response is not delivered until two days later.

Fifth, agents forget to calendar the seller's response window after a buyer's inspection objection is delivered. Section 10 of the MAR Purchase Agreement creates a secondary deadline—typically a negotiated number of days—for the seller to respond. If the seller misses that window, the agreement may automatically terminate depending on the version of the form used. The buyer's agent who fails to track this loses the ability to advise the buyer on next steps.

Agents who rely on memory rather than systems to track these dates are setting themselves up for exactly this kind of failure. Building a [weekly pipeline review system](/blog/weekly-pipeline-review-system) catches deadline drift before it becomes a compliance issue.

## Michigan Buyer Contingency Deadlines 2026: What Brokers Must Audit

Brokers carry supervisory liability under MCL 339.2512a. If an agent under your supervision misses a contingency deadline and harms a client, LARA can discipline you for inadequate oversight. The standard is whether you had "reasonable policies and procedures" in place to prevent the error.

| Audit Item | Frequency | What to Look For |
|------------|-----------|------------------|
| Signed purchase agreements | Within 24 hours of execution | All deadline blanks filled in (no empty fields) |
| Contingency tracking log | Weekly | Every active deal has documented deadlines |
| Delivery confirmations | Per contingency action | Written proof of timely delivery (email receipt, fax confirmation, signed acknowledgment) |
| Deadline extension amendments | As they occur | Properly executed MAR Addendum with all signatures |
| File completeness at closing | Before disbursement | All contingency actions documented in chronological order |

Brokers should implement a system where every executed purchase agreement is reviewed within one business day. The review should confirm that every deadline blank in Sections 10 through 13 contains a specific date or a calculable "days from acceptance" figure. A blank field is an ambiguity that invites litigation.

**Broker liability is not theoretical.** In 2023, LARA issued a consent order against a Michigan brokerage that failed to supervise an agent who repeatedly missed financing contingency deadlines across multiple transactions. The brokerage paid a $15,000 fine and the designated broker completed 30 hours of remedial education. The lesson: your agents' deadline failures become your compliance failures.

## Protecting Yourself: Documentation Standards That Hold Up

The single best defense against a LARA complaint or civil claim is contemporaneous documentation. Every contingency action—delivery of an inspection response, receipt of a financing denial, exercise of a termination right—should be documented with a timestamp, the delivery method, and confirmation of receipt.

Michigan courts apply the "mailbox rule" to determine when notice is effective: notice sent by an authorized method is effective upon dispatch, not receipt, unless the contract states otherwise. The MAR Purchase Agreement's notice provision (Section 21) generally follows this rule, but agents should still obtain delivery confirmation to eliminate disputes.

> Under Section 21 of the MAR Purchase Agreement, "notice shall be deemed given when deposited in the United States mail, postage prepaid, certified, return receipt requested, or when transmitted by facsimile or electronic means to the number or address set forth in this Agreement."

That language means an email sent at 11:45 PM on the deadline date satisfies the requirement—but only if the email address used matches what appears in the contract. Sending it to a different address, even one the listing agent uses regularly, creates a delivery dispute.
