## Texas Continuing Education Requirements 2026: The Full Breakdown

The Texas Real Estate Commission (TREC) does not give grace periods, does not send reminders by certified mail, and does not care that you were busy closing three deals the week your license lapsed. Understanding Texas continuing education requirements 2026 is not optional—it is the difference between practicing legally and facing fines that start at $5,000 per violation. Every renewal cycle, agents across the state make preventable errors that cost them income, reputation, and in some cases their license entirely.

TREC governs all real estate licensing under the Texas Occupations Code, Chapter 1101. The rules around continuing education fall primarily under Title 22, Texas Administrative Code, Chapter 535, which spells out hours, topics, deadlines, and penalties with zero ambiguity. If you have not reviewed these rules since your last renewal, this is the year to do it.

## Hour Requirements and Mandatory Topics

**Active sales agents must complete 18 hours of continuing education every two-year renewal cycle.** This breaks down into two categories: mandatory courses and elective hours. The mandatory component is non-negotiable—skip it, and TREC will not renew your license regardless of how many elective hours you stacked up.

The 18 hours split as follows: 4 hours of Legal Update I (Course No. 33601), 4 hours of Legal Update II (Course No. 33602), and 10 hours of elective CE from any TREC-approved education provider. Brokers face the same 18-hour requirement but must also complete a Broker Responsibility course during their first renewal cycle after receiving a broker license.

| Requirement | Sales Agents | Brokers |
|---|---|---|
| Legal Update I (33601) | 4 hours (mandatory) | 4 hours (mandatory) |
| Legal Update II (33602) | 4 hours (mandatory) | 4 hours (mandatory) |
| Elective CE | 10 hours | 10 hours |
| Broker Responsibility | Not required | Required (first renewal cycle) |
| Total Hours | 18 hours | 18 hours + Broker Responsibility |

Legal Update I and II cover changes in Texas real estate law, TREC rules, and contract forms. For 2026, expect these courses to address the latest TREC-promulgated contract revisions, updates to the [One to Four Family Residential Contract (TREC Form 20-17)](https://www.trec.texas.gov/forms/contracts), and recent enforcement actions that signal where the commission is focusing scrutiny.

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

A lapsed license does not just mean you cannot list a property. It means every transaction you touch while unlicensed is potentially voidable, every commission you earn is subject to disgorgement, and your broker's entire operation faces regulatory exposure.

Under Section 1101.701 of the Texas Occupations Code, TREC can impose administrative penalties of up to $5,000 for each violation. Practicing without a valid license—even if the lapse was unintentional—qualifies as a violation. The commission can also suspend or revoke your license, require additional education, or issue a formal reprimand that becomes part of your permanent TREC record.

**The financial fallout extends beyond fines.** If a buyer or seller discovers their agent was operating on an inactive license during a transaction, they may have grounds to rescind the contract or sue for damages. Title companies that catch the issue will halt closings. Lenders will flag the file. The downstream cost of a single missed CE deadline can easily exceed $50,000 when you factor in lost commissions, legal fees, and deal cancellations—a scenario not unlike the chaos described in [the true cost of a blown closing](/blog/true-cost-of-a-blown-closing).

## Common Mistakes Agents Make With CE Compliance

Hundreds of Texas agents run into the same preventable errors every cycle. Here are the five most common—and none of them require a regulatory genius to avoid.

**First: completing CE hours but failing to verify the provider reported them to TREC.** You can sit through 18 hours of coursework and still show zero hours on your TREC profile if the provider failed to upload your completion records. Always check your license status on the TREC website within 10 business days of finishing a course.

Second: taking Legal Update I or II from a prior cycle. TREC updates these courses regularly, and each version is cycle-specific. Completing the 2024 Legal Update I in 2026 does not satisfy the 2026 requirement. The course numbers remain the same, but the curriculum content must match your current renewal period.

Third: assuming that a license on inactive status can be renewed without penalty. If your license has been inactive for more than two years, you cannot simply pay a fee and complete CE. TREC may require you to retake the licensing exam or complete additional qualifying coursework under Section 535.55 of the Texas Administrative Code.

Fourth: confusing SAE (Sales Agent Apprentice Education) hours with CE hours. New agents often believe the 90 hours of post-license SAE satisfy their CE requirement. They do not. SAE and CE are separate obligations tracked in separate systems.

Fifth: missing the actual renewal deadline because you confused your license expiration date with your CE completion deadline. CE must be completed before you submit your renewal application. Submitting a renewal without completed CE triggers an automatic denial.

## What Brokers Need to Audit and Enforce

Sponsoring brokers carry direct liability for agents operating under their umbrella without valid licenses. TREC Rule 535.2 makes this explicit: a broker must maintain a system to verify that every sponsored agent holds an active license in good standing. The commission has shown willingness to sanction brokers who plead ignorance.

| Broker Audit Item | Frequency | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Agent license status verification | Monthly minimum | TREC online license lookup |
| CE completion confirmation | 90 days before each agent's expiration | Provider records + TREC profile |
| Broker Responsibility course tracking | Each broker renewal cycle | TREC transcript |
| Sponsorship termination filing (Form BRSP-4) | Within 3 days of termination | TREC form submission |
| Record retention of agent CE documentation | 4 years | TAC 535.2 |

**Brokers should build a calendar-based audit system rather than relying on agents to self-report.** TREC's online license search allows you to verify any agent's status in seconds—there is no excuse for discovering a lapse after a transaction is underway. Many brokerages now run monthly compliance sweeps, flagging any agent whose renewal date falls within 90 days and requiring proof of CE enrollment.

When an agent departs your brokerage, you must file the termination with TREC using the appropriate sponsorship termination process within the mandated timeframe. Failure to terminate sponsorship leaves you potentially liable for that agent's actions at their new firm until the paperwork clears. For brokers managing multiple agents, this is exactly the type of deadline that slips through cracks—[tracking multiple active deals](/blog/track-multiple-active-deals) is hard enough without adding compliance calendars to the mix.

## How CE Intersects With Transaction Compliance

Continuing education is not just a licensing checkbox—it directly affects your ability to handle transactions competently. Legal Update I and II cover changes to TREC-promulgated forms that agents are required to use in most residential transactions. If you skipped the course, you may not know that form language changed, that a new addendum exists, or that a disclosure requirement was added.

Texas agents must use TREC-promulgated forms unless the transaction qualifies for an exemption under TREC Rule 537.11. Using an outdated form version because you missed the Legal Update course that explained the revision is not a defense. TREC has disciplined agents for using superseded contract forms, and opposing counsel in disputes will absolutely raise the issue.

This connects directly to broader compliance awareness. Agents who stay current on CE are less likely to [miss critical deadlines](/blog/why-agents-miss-deadlines) in active transactions because the training reinforces procedural discipline. The agents who treat CE as a box to check are the same ones who forget option period expirations and amendment deadlines.

## Texas Continuing Education Requirements 2026: Deadlines and Renewal Process

Your renewal deadline is tied to your individual license expiration date—not a universal calendar date. TREC sends renewal notices approximately 90 days before expiration, but receiving (or not receiving) that notice does not change your deadline. The onus is entirely on you.

> "A license holder is responsible for timely renewing the license regardless of whether the license holder receives a renewal notice from the commission." — Texas Administrative Code, Title 22, Section 535.56

Renewal applications are submitted online through TREC's website. You cannot submit the application until all 18 hours of CE appear on your TREC education transcript. The renewal fee for sales agents is currently $110 for a two-year period. Late renewals within 90 days of expiration carry an additional penalty fee equal to one and one-half times the renewal fee.

If you pass the 90-day late window, your license expires entirely. Reinstatement after full expiration requires completing all CE hours, paying increased fees, and potentially meeting additional TREC requirements depending on how long the license remained expired.
