## New York Continuing Education Requirements 2026: The Full Breakdown

**If you hold an active real estate license in New York, meeting the New York continuing education requirements 2026 cycle is not optional — it is a condition of keeping your license active and your commissions legally earned.** The New York Department of State (DOS), through its Division of Licensing Services, administers all real estate licensing under Article 12-A of the Real Property Law. Every salesperson and associate broker must complete 22.5 hours of approved continuing education (CE) during each two-year renewal period.

The 2026 renewal cycle brings no reduction in hour totals but does reflect updated guidance on fair housing instruction and ethical practice. Agents who last renewed in 2024 are now inside their compliance window and should be tracking completion dates closely. Missing the deadline doesn't just mean a lapse — it means every deal you touch during that gap carries legal exposure for you and your supervising broker.

## Mandatory Course Topics and Hour Requirements

New York's CE structure is more prescriptive than many states. The 22.5 total hours must include specific mandatory modules, and completing generic electives alone will not satisfy the requirement.

Here is how the hours break down for the current renewal cycle:

| Category | Required Hours | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| Fair Housing and Discrimination | 3.0 | Must cover NYS Human Rights Law and federal FHA |
| Agency Law | 2.0 | Includes disclosure form obligations |
| Ethics and Business Conduct | 1.0 | Separate from fair housing content |
| Recent Legal Matters | 2.0 | Statutory and regulatory updates |
| Electives (approved topics) | 14.5 | Any DOS-approved subject matter |
| **Total** | **22.5** | Must be completed before renewal date |

The fair housing module must address both the federal Fair Housing Act and the New York State Human Rights Law (Executive Law, Article 15). This dual requirement trips up agents who take courses designed for other states and assume they transfer. They don't — not unless the provider holds New York DOS approval and the curriculum covers state-specific protected classes, which in New York include additional categories like source of income and military status.

## How to Verify Provider Approval and Track Completion

**Every course you take must come from a school or instructor holding current approval from the Division of Licensing Services.** The DOS maintains a searchable database of approved real estate schools on its website. If a provider cannot show you their DOS approval number, do not assume the hours will count.

Upon completing each course, your provider submits an electronic certificate to the DOS. You should also receive Form DOS-1251 (the CE completion certificate) for your personal records. Retain these certificates for at least six years — the DOS can audit your file at any point, and reconstructing records from a defunct school is a nightmare agents regularly face.

Your renewal application is submitted on Form DOS-0032, and the system cross-references your reported CE hours. If there is a mismatch — for example, a provider failed to report or you took an unapproved course — the renewal will stall or be denied. Tracking your own hours independently of the DOS system is the only reliable safety net. Many agents who [miss critical deadlines](/blog/why-agents-miss-deadlines) do so because they trusted a provider to handle reporting and never followed up.

## What Happens When You Fail to Comply

The consequences of missing your CE deadline are immediate and financially painful. Your license moves to "lapsed" status on the day after expiration. You cannot list properties, show homes, negotiate contracts, or collect commissions while lapsed.

If you complete a transaction during a lapse — even unknowingly — the buyer or seller can seek rescission of the contract. Under Real Property Law Section 440-a, operating without a valid license is a misdemeanor. The DOS can also impose civil fines up to $1,000 per violation and refer cases to the Attorney General's office for additional enforcement action.

Reinstatement within one year of expiration requires completing all outstanding CE, submitting a new Form DOS-0032, and paying the standard renewal fee plus a late penalty. If more than one year passes, you must retake the 75-hour qualifying course and pass the state licensing exam again — effectively starting from scratch. For brokers, the liability extends further: if an associated salesperson was operating on a lapsed license, the broker faces disciplinary proceedings and potential loss of their own license under 19 NYCRR Part 175.

## Common Mistakes Agents Make With New York CE

The same errors surface every renewal cycle. Recognizing them now prevents expensive scrambles later.

**First, agents assume out-of-state CE transfers automatically.** It does not. New York has no blanket reciprocity for continuing education. Even if you hold licenses in Connecticut or New Jersey, only courses approved by the New York DOS count toward your New York renewal. The subject matter may overlap, but the approval must be state-specific.

Second, agents stack all 22.5 hours into the final month before their renewal date. Providers experience enrollment surges in the weeks before major renewal deadlines, and courses fill up. If you cannot secure a seat, the DOS will not grant an extension. There is no grace period written into Article 12-A.

Third, agents confuse the salesperson renewal cycle with the broker renewal cycle. While both require 22.5 hours, the renewal dates are tied to your original license issuance, not to a universal calendar deadline. Your personal renewal date appears on your license and in the eAccessNY portal. Assuming your date matches a colleague's is a common path to a lapse.

Fourth, agents fail to retain Form DOS-1251 certificates. When a provider goes out of business or loses records, you have no backup documentation. The DOS places the burden of proof on the licensee, not the school.

Fifth, some agents complete courses from providers whose approval expired mid-year. A school that was approved in January may lose approval by June if it fails to meet DOS standards. Always verify approval status at the time you enroll, not based on a brochure printed six months earlier.

## What Brokers Need to Audit and Enforce

Under 19 NYCRR Section 175.21, a supervising broker bears direct responsibility for the conduct and compliance of every salesperson associated with their office. This includes verifying that each agent's license is current.

| Broker Audit Action | Frequency | Source |
|---------------------|-----------|--------|
| Verify agent license status via eAccessNY | Quarterly | DOS online portal |
| Collect copies of Form DOS-1251 from agents | As courses are completed | Agent-provided |
| Confirm renewal applications are submitted | 60 days before each agent's expiration | Internal calendar |
| Document audit results in personnel files | Ongoing | Brokerage policy |
| Remove lapsed agents from MLS and marketing | Immediately upon expiration | MLS rules + DOS regulation |

**Brokers who treat license auditing as an annual chore are exposing themselves to regulatory action.** The DOS has increased enforcement actions against brokerages that allow lapsed agents to continue transacting. A single deal closed by an unlicensed salesperson can unravel the entire transaction and trigger an audit of the brokerage's full roster.

Similarly, maintaining a [transaction handoff checklist](/blog/transaction-handoff-checklist) that includes a license verification step prevents deals from proceeding under an invalid license.

## The 2026 Fair Housing Update You Cannot Ignore

The New York DOS issued updated guidance in late 2025 clarifying that the 3-hour fair housing requirement must include instruction on source-of-income discrimination, which has been a growing enforcement priority under the Human Rights Law. This reflects a broader push by the New York State Division of Human Rights to address housing discrimination tied to Section 8 vouchers and other government subsidies.

Agents should confirm that their chosen fair housing course explicitly covers this topic. Courses that only address the seven federal protected classes without touching New York's expanded list will not satisfy the requirement. The [New York Department of State Division of Licensing Services](https://dos.ny.gov/licensing-real-estate-salespeople-and-brokers) posts approved course catalogs that indicate which mandatory modules each course fulfills.

## Planning Your CE Completion Timeline

Spreading your 22.5 hours across the two-year cycle is the most reliable strategy. Completing one mandatory module every quarter leaves room for scheduling conflicts, illness, or provider issues without putting your license at risk.

Online courses approved by the DOS offer flexibility, but verify that the format is accepted for all module types. New York currently permits all 22.5 hours to be completed online through approved platforms, though in-person options remain available for agents who prefer classroom settings. The key is confirming DOS approval regardless of delivery method.
