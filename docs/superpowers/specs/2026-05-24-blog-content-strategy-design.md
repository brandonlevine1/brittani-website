# Blog Content Strategy: "Regulatory Surface Area" — 3 Posts/Day

## Overview

A scalable content engine that publishes 3 AI-drafted, human-reviewed blog posts per day to the Britanni AI marketing site. The strategy exploits the 50-state regulatory landscape of residential real estate to generate high-intent, low-competition SEO content while mixing in operational and brand-authority posts that drive conversions.

**Target audiences:** Residential real estate agents (primary), brokers/team leads (secondary)

**Goals per post type:**
- Post Type 1 (State-specific regulatory): SEO traffic — rank for long-tail searches agents make mid-deal
- Post Type 2 (Operational/how-to): Lead generation — demonstrate Britanni's value, drive trial signups
- Post Type 3 (Industry trend/opinion): Brand authority — establish Britanni as a trusted voice brokers share

---

## Daily Cadence: The Three Post Types

### Type 1: State-Specific Regulatory/Legal Post (SEO)

**Purpose:** Capture search traffic from agents who Google state-specific legal questions while actively working deals.

**Why this works:** Agents don't search "real estate disclosure requirements" — they search "Georgia seller disclosure requirements 2026" or "does California require lead paint disclosure for condos built after 1978." These long-tail queries have low competition, high intent, and a natural connection to Britanni's contract-analysis feature.

**Topic categories (per state):**

| Category | Example titles | Est. posts per state |
|----------|---------------|---------------------|
| Seller disclosure requirements | "Texas Seller Disclosure Requirements: What Must Be Disclosed in 2026" | 2-3 |
| Buyer contingency timelines | "Arizona Inspection Contingency Deadlines: What Agents Need to Know" | 2-3 |
| Contract & earnest money rules | "Florida Earnest Money Rules: Deposit Amounts, Timelines & Disputes" | 2-3 |
| Building code impacts on transactions | "New York Energy Code Updates 2026: What Realtors Need to Tell Buyers" | 1-2 |
| License & commission regulations | "Colorado Real Estate Commission Rules: Dual Agency, Disclosure & Compliance" | 1-2 |
| HOA/condo-specific regulations | "Illinois Condo Resale Certificate Requirements for Agents" | 1-2 |
| Title & closing procedures | "Georgia Attorney-State Closing Requirements: What Out-of-State Agents Miss" | 1-2 |
| Wire fraud & escrow regulations | "California Escrow Holder Requirements: Licensing, Timelines & Agent Duties" | 1 |
| New laws & regulatory updates | "2026 Washington State Real Estate Law Changes: Impact on Active Contracts" | 1-2 (annual refresh) |
| Property condition & environmental | "Ohio Radon Disclosure Laws: Agent Obligations and Liability" | 1-2 |

**Estimated total capacity:** 50 states x ~15 posts average = **750+ unique posts** before any topic is repeated. With annual regulatory updates and new legislation, this is effectively infinite.

**Template structure:**
```
Title: "[State] [Topic]: [What Agents Need to Know in 2026]"
Meta description: Actionable summary with state name + year for freshness signal
H1: Match title exactly
Intro: What this applies to, who needs to know, why it matters RIGHT NOW
Body sections:
  - The current requirements (with specific statutes/forms cited)
  - Common mistakes agents make
  - How this affects transaction timelines
  - What brokers should ensure their teams know
CTA: "Britanni AI automatically tracks [relevant feature] — start your free trial"
Word count: 1,200-1,800 words
```

**SEO signals to include:**
- State name in title, H1, URL slug, and first paragraph
- Current year in title (freshness signal)
- Specific form numbers/statute references (establishes expertise)
- Schema.org Article + BreadcrumbList markup
- Internal links to related state posts and Britanni features

**Purpose:** Show agents and brokers how to solve workflow problems they're currently experiencing. Naturally demonstrates Britanni's value.

**Topic categories:**

| Tech adoption | "How to Evaluate TC Software: The Broker's Decision Framework" |

**Template structure:**
```
Title: Action-oriented ("How to...", "The X-Step System for...", "Why Your Team...")
Meta description: Promise a specific outcome
Intro: Validate the pain point (show you understand the problem)
Body: Step-by-step solution OR problem/solution pairs
  - Use numbered steps, checklists, or comparison tables
  - Include specific numbers (time saved, error rates, cost comparisons)
Bridge: "Here's how this works in practice with automation..."
CTA: Feature-specific Britanni CTA tied to the post's topic
Word count: 1,000-1,500 words
```

**Conversion elements:**
- Every post references a specific Britanni feature (not generic "try our tool")
- Include a "manual vs. automated" comparison where natural
- End with a single, specific CTA (not multiple competing CTAs)

### Weekly Planning

Each week, plan the next 21 posts (7 days x 3 posts). The split:

- **7 state-specific posts:** Rotate through states systematically. Priority order: largest agent populations first (California, Texas, Florida, New York, Pennsylvania, Illinois, Ohio, Georgia, North Carolina, Michigan — then expand).
- **7 operational posts:** Drawn from the topic categories above. Prioritize topics that align with Britanni features currently in production.
- **7 industry/opinion posts:** Tied to current events, data releases, regulatory changes, or evergreen hot takes.

### Content Calendar Seeding (First 30 Days)

**Week 1 — Foundation states (CA, TX, FL):**
- Day 1: CA seller disclosure requirements / How to track deadlines at scale / AI in real estate: hype vs. reality
- Day 2: TX earnest money rules / Email sequences for new contracts / NAR settlement one year later
- Day 3: FL inspection contingency timelines / Broker's guide to TC software / The coming disclosure law tightening
- Day 4: CA lead paint disclosure for condos / 5 contract defects that kill deals / Stop calling templates "AI"
- Day 5: TX option period explained / Inspection scheduling without phone tag / Agent turnover's hidden cost
- Day 6: FL HOA estoppel certificate requirements / Monthly compliance audit checklist / Will AI replace TCs?
- Day 7: CA agency disclosure timeline / Training new agents on timelines / We analyzed 10K transactions

**Weeks 2-4:** Expand to NY, PA, IL, OH, GA, NC, MI while continuing operational and opinion cadence.

## SEO Architecture

### URL Structure

```
/blog/[state]-[topic-slug]          → State-specific (Type 1)
/blog/[topic-slug]                  → Operational (Type 2)
/blog/[topic-slug]                  → Opinion (Type 3)
```

Examples:
- `/blog/california-seller-disclosure-requirements-2026`
- `/blog/how-to-track-15-active-deals`
- `/blog/nar-settlement-year-one`

### Internal Linking Strategy

- Every Type 1 post links to 2-3 other posts for the same state
- Every Type 1 post links to the relevant Britanni feature page
- Every Type 2 post links to 1-2 Type 1 posts as "state-specific examples"
- Create state hub pages once 5+ posts exist for a state (e.g., `/blog/california` lists all CA posts)

### Tag Taxonomy

```
Primary tags (one per post):
- seller-disclosure, buyer-contingencies, earnest-money, building-codes,
  licensing-regulations, hoa-condo, title-closing, wire-fraud-escrow,
  regulatory-updates, property-condition

Secondary tags:
- State name (e.g., "california", "texas")
- Audience (e.g., "brokers", "agents")
- Content type (e.g., "guide", "checklist", "opinion", "analysis")
```

### Schema Markup

Every post should include:
- `Article` schema with author, datePublished, dateModified
- `BreadcrumbList` (Home > Blog > [Post])
- `FAQPage` schema where applicable (Type 1 posts with Q&A format sections)

## Tone & Style Guide

### Voice Principles

1. **Expert but approachable** — cite specific statutes but explain them in plain English
2. **Practitioner perspective** — write as someone who has managed transactions, not an outsider looking in
3. **Direct** — lead with the answer, then explain. No "in this article we will explore..."
4. **Opinionated when appropriate** — Type 3 posts should take clear stances
5. **Conversion-aware but not salesy** — Britanni CTAs are earned by delivering value, not shoe-horned in

### What to avoid

- Generic filler ("In today's fast-paced real estate market...")
- Hedging everything ("This may vary depending on circumstances" — be specific instead)
- Overusing AI/tech buzzwords in Type 1 posts (these should read as regulatory expertise, not product marketing)
- Duplicating content across states with only the state name swapped (each state genuinely has different rules)

### Author attribution

- Type 1: "Britanni AI Team" (signals institutional knowledge)
- Type 2: Rotate between founders (Jack Brighenti, Brandon Levine, Brittany Brighenti)
- Type 3: Specific founder byline (builds personal brand + thought leadership)

## AI Drafting Prompts (Template)

### Type 1 prompt template:

```
Write a blog post for real estate agents about [TOPIC] in [STATE].

Target keyword: "[STATE] [topic phrase] [year]"
Word count: 1,200-1,800 words
Audience: Licensed real estate agents and their brokers in [STATE]

Requirements:
- Cite specific state statutes, forms, or regulatory bodies by name
- Include the specific form numbers agents use (e.g., "TAR Form T-47" for Texas)
- Explain what happens if an agent fails to comply (liability, deal cancellation, fines)
- Include a "Common Mistakes" section with 3-5 specific errors
- Include a "What Brokers Should Know" section (2-3 paragraphs)
- End with a Britanni AI CTA tied to [RELEVANT FEATURE]
- Tone: authoritative, practical, direct
- Do NOT include generic filler or obvious statements
- Format as Astro markdown with frontmatter (title, description, publishDate, author, readTime, tags)
```

### Type 2 prompt template:

```
Write an operational how-to blog post for real estate agents about [TOPIC].

Target keyword: "[action phrase] [context]"
Word count: 1,000-1,500 words
Audience: Agents managing 5-15 active deals + brokers managing agent teams

Requirements:
- Lead with the pain point (quantify it: time wasted, error rates, liability exposure)
- Provide a concrete, step-by-step solution
- Include specific numbers or comparisons where possible
- Bridge naturally to how Britanni automates this workflow
- End with a single specific CTA (free trial, feature page, or demo)
- Tone: helpful, experienced, solution-oriented
- Format as Astro markdown with frontmatter
```

### Type 3 prompt template:

```
Write an opinion/analysis blog post about [TOPIC] in the real estate industry.

Target keyword: "[trend/event] [impact/analysis]"
Word count: 800-1,200 words
Audience: Brokers, team leads, and senior agents who follow industry trends

Requirements:
- Take a clear stance in the first paragraph — no burying the thesis
- Support with data, specific examples, or logical argument
- Reference specific industry events, regulations, or data sources
- Acknowledge the strongest counterargument, then address it
- End with an actionable takeaway
- CTA: soft (newsletter, "see how we're building for this")
- Tone: confident, informed, slightly contrarian
- Byline: [FOUNDER NAME]
- Format as Astro markdown with frontmatter
```

- If Type 1 posts aren't indexing after 30 days: add more internal links, check for thin content
- If Type 2 CTAs aren't converting: test different CTA placements and copy
- If Type 3 posts get no social traction: shift toward more data-driven or contrarian angles

## Technical Implementation Notes

### Astro content schema (already supports this)

The existing `content.config.ts` schema handles all needed fields. No schema changes required. Tags are already supported as `z.array(z.string()).optional()`.

### Potential enhancements for scale

1. **State hub pages** — Create `/blog/states/[state].astro` that auto-generates a page listing all posts for that state once 5+ posts exist
2. **Tag pages** — Create `/blog/tag/[tag].astro` for browsing by topic
3. **Related posts component** — Show 2-3 related posts at the bottom of each article (same state or same topic)
4. **Blog pagination** — Current blog index will need pagination at 50+ posts
5. **Sitemap generation** — Ensure all blog posts are in the XML sitemap for crawl efficiency
6. **RSS feed** — Add for subscribers and syndication

### Content file naming convention

```
src/content/blog/
  [state]-[topic-slug].md          → Type 1
  [action-slug].md                 → Type 2
  [topic-slug].md                  → Type 3
```

## Risk & Mitigation

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| Legal inaccuracy in state-specific posts | Liability, reputation damage | Human review MUST fact-check statute citations; add disclaimer footer |
| Content fatigue / repetition | Readers tune out | Vary formats (listicles, deep-dives, checklists, Q&A); monitor time-on-page |
| Google thin-content penalty | SEO collapse | Ensure each state post has genuinely unique content, not just state-name swaps |
| 3/day unsustainable at quality bar | Quality drops, brand damage | Build a 2-week buffer; drop to 2/day if review queue backs up |
| Regulatory changes invalidate posts | Outdated info damages trust | Schedule quarterly audits; add "Last updated" dates; prioritize high-traffic posts |

### Legal disclaimer

All Type 1 posts should include a standard footer disclaimer:
> "This article is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Real estate regulations change frequently — verify current requirements with your state's real estate commission or a licensed attorney."
